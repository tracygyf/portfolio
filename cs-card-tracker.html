Card tracker

  • Problem
  • Process
  • Outcome

Knowing where your card is, before you ask

Real-time card delivery tracking that reduced delivery-related support enquiries by 40% by giving users visibility at every step of the journey.

Role
Product Designer
Platform
Mobile App
Impact
↓ 40% support tickets
Problem

An estimated date isn't the same as knowing

Previously, users received only an estimated delivery date and a single email notification on the day their card went out for delivery. No tracking, no real-time updates, no way to know where things stood.

This lack of visibility led to avoidable anxiety, unnecessary support contacts, and a persistent "activate your card" prompt that meant nothing to users still waiting for it to arrive.

📅

No real-time tracking

Users only had an estimated date and no way to see where their card actually was in the delivery process.

💬

Avoidable support load

Uncertainty drove users to contact support for basic status updates.

Process

Finding the right level of detail

I mapped out the full card ordering process, then explored different ways to surface status to users. Two directions were tested:

Full tracker layout

It had comprehensive tracking steps, but it occupied too much screen space and pushed important actions below the fold.

Message banner

A simplified banner showing the current step with a popup for full details. Much cleaner, but messaging needed refinement.

Outcome

A minimalist banner. A meaningful difference.

The final design fused the 2 ideas together: A compact banner clearly showing the card's current status and estimated delivery date. Users who want the full breakdown can open a modal without cluttering the card page for those who don't.

The persistent "activate your card" prompt was also removed entirely for users still awaiting delivery and only shows once the tracker was in its "Card shipped" status. This eliminated a confusing, premature call to action.

Post-launch, we've seen a 40% drop in delivery-related support enquiries.
